EcoCycle SkyScrapper

location: Tel Aviv, Israel

Design: 2024

Final project Master degree

EcoCycle Skyscraper explores the future of high-rise architecture in the context of climate change, resource scarcity, and rapid urbanization. It proposes a new model for skyscraper design one that prioritizes longevity, sustainability, and adaptability to future needs. Rather than tearing down and rebuilding, the design focuses on preserving the structural core, integrating renewable technologies, and allowing for flexible use over time. Located in Tel Aviv’s Savidor Center and aligned with National Plan 70, the skyscraper addresses the growing need for urban density and transit-oriented development. It’s designed to connect both vertically and horizontally with its surroundings, maintaining a human scale while enabling future changes without major demolition. EcoCycle Skyscrapers reimagines the skyscraper as a living, evolving part of the city. It is built to last, environmentally responsible, and ready to meet the challenges of tomorrow.
