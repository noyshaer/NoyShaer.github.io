Eco Urbanism

location: 56 Rokach Street, Ramat Gan, Israel

Design: 2022

The project focuses on eco-urbanism, connecting the city, people, and nature through architecture. It creates a gradient between private and shared spaces, encouraging interaction and community life. A bike path links Napoleon Hill with Narkisim Boulevard, complemented by a public plaza beside the building. The ground floor opens to the neighborhood with commercial spaces and green areas that invite engagement. “Ecolopes” integrate nature into the structure through ecological pools, green columns, and a wildlife bridge. Pyramid-shaped tiles host plants and blur the boundaries between the natural and built environments. The building offers study rooms, terraces, and a gym to support a vibrant student community. Sunlight analysis guided the planting of herbs and succulents, ensuring ecological harmony with the surroundings.
