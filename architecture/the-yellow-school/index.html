The Yellow School

location: Turin, Italy

Design: 2022

student exchange program in Turin, Italy

The school building is composed of five boxes, each designated for a different function: classrooms, a library, computer rooms, a dining hall, a gallery, and a gym. The connection between the volumes is made through a wide, open central space with roof openings that allow natural light and ventilation, creating an inner courtyard linking all parts of the building. The roof system and its facades generate a sequence of open and shaded spaces, including exterior staircases that connect the floors and enable dynamic use of the structure. In addition, dynamic cubes were integrated, serving as shading elements, playful features for students, and a system for collecting rainwater. The result is a multi-layered building that combines architecture, sustainability, and a sense of community belonging.
